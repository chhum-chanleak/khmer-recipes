Fish Salad With Shredded Coconut

Description

This starter dish is popular for special occasions in Cambodia: wedding receptions, dinner parties, or big family gatherings. This should be served fresh immediately after dressing. It is an unusual combination of ceviche fish and coconut meat. !e English translation of this dish’s name means "ceviche by the riverside” so consider serving the dish for a boating party.

Ingredients

  • 50 g/1.8 oz Dried shrimp

  • 40 g/1⁄4 cup Rice

  • 100 g/3.5 oz Shredded coconut meat

  • 20 g/0.7 oz Bitter orange zest

  • 50 g/1.8 oz Roasted peanuts

  • 100 g/3.5 oz Pickled scallion heads

  • 30 ml/2 tbsp Brine from pickled scallion heads

  • 25 g/0.9 oz Galangal

  • 30 g/1 oz Shallot

  • 20 ml/4 tsp Bitter orange juice

  • 50 ml/31⁄2 tbsp Lime juice

  • 30 ml/2 tbsp Fish sauce

  • 50 g/22⁄3 tbsp Palm sugar

Cooking instructions

  1. Rinse the rice, soak in water for about 10 mins, then drain well. Heat a ok or a large skillet over medium heat. As the pan is heating, add the rice, then stir constantly until the rice is an even, dark golden brown and smells very fragrant, about 5 mins. Remove the roasted rice to a bowl and let it cool down. Transfer the rice into a spice grinder or a mortar and pestle; "nely grind the rice, then sift in a "ne mesh-strainer to remove any large chunks. Set aside. !inly slice the shallots lengthwise. Finely chop the galangal, pickled scallion heads, chili pepper, and bitter orange zest. Grind the roasted peanuts coarsely. Heat another frying pan over low heat, add the shredded coconut meat and cook, stirring, for about 2-3ns,set aside.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the lime juice, bitter lime juice, palm sugar, brine from the pickled scallion heads, and "sh sauce, stir until horoughly mixed.

  3. In another mixing bowl, add the dried shrimp, shredded coconut meat, all the spices, and salad dressing, stir together well, then add the rice powder, and stir again. Top with the roasted peanuts.

  4. To serve, place the salad on a large plate. Each guest should spoon the lad on to their own individual small plate, wrap it in leafy greens in bite size portions and enjoy.
