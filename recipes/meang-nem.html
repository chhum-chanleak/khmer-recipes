Meang Nem

Description

The dish is named because of the color from the snow peas which is similar to an emerald. We mostly see Chinese dishes using the snow peas either in soups or stir fries, and it is rare to use the peas in Cambodian cuisine; especially, with the paste which includes galangal, lemongrass, ka "r lime leaves, Asian basil leaves, and shrimp paste. !e taste from coconut cream combined with the paste from different ingredients, you will not be disappointed by spending time cooking it. Not only does it provide an unforgettable taste, the peas have rich nutritional value with $ber, protein, calcium, iron, and other vitamins and minerals, which helps prevent diabetes and reduce premature aging.

Ingredients

  • 500 g/1.1lb Chicken meat

  • 10 g/0.7tbsp Shrimp paste

  • 225 g/0.5lb Green cubanelle peppers

  • 2 pounds Snow peas (should be mature ones so the peas are not too small)

  • 100 ml/0.4cup Coconut cream

  • 500 ml/2cups Coconut milk

  • 1 bunch Khmer basil

  • 5 pcs Ka"r limes Leaves

  • 2 g/0.6tsp Ka"r lime zest

  • 25 g/0.9oz Lemongrass

  • 5 g/0.2oz Cilantro root

  • 10 g/0.3oz Galangal

  • 20 g/0.7oz Garlic

  • 40 g/1.4oz Shallots

  • 20 g/1tbsp Palm sugar

  • 2 g/1tsp Ground black pepper

  • 2 g/0.3tsp Salt

  • 15 ml/1tbsp Fish sauce

Cooking instructions

  1. Slice the chicken into half inch cubes. Remove peas from pods and set aside. Thinly slice lemongrass, shallots, and garlic. Finely chop cilantro root and kaffir lime zest. Pound all of these ingredients with shrimp paste, salt, and pepper into a fine paste. Julienne the cubanelle peppers. Chiffonade the Khmer basil and kaffir lime leaves.

  2. Boil the coconut cream in a medium cooking pot. Add paste and cook until the paste is aromatic, add chicken, stir evenly, simmer for 3 minutes, and add coconut milk. Cook until it boils. Add peas, $sh sauce, and palm sugar. Bring back to a boil and turn o # heat. Finish with the cubanelle peppers and turn ob the heat.

  3. To serve, place the soup in a serving bowl, garnish with the Khmer basil and kaffir lime leaves.
